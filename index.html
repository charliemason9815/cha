Top 16 Real Estate Investing Podcasts for Beginners

I learned from my father that investing in real estate is a great way to build passive income and wealth. On a government salary, he was able to purchase ten rental properties and fill them with tenants for additional monthly income. 

From a young age, I knew I was going to be a real estate investor, too. So it’s no surprise when my family and I moved to Dubai for three years, I became a landlord and rented out our home instead of selling it. I knew that in the long run the passive income would be better for our family in addition to holding an asset for my children. Then, when we came back, I used the cash we saved up to purchase two more properties. 

To learn what I needed to be successful, I talked with other real estate investors, took some courses, read books and did a lot of research. Podcasts are a great way to learn about real estate investing on the go. Check out this list of top real estate investing podcasts. If you are a newbie to the field, listen to a few different experiences and perspectives. Take notes and learn from other people’s mistakes to find tactics and paths that will work for you. 

Top Real Estate Investing Podcasts

1. Bigger Pockets Podcast

Bigger Pockets is the best place for beginner real estate investors to start. Founded by Joshua Dorkin, Bigger Pockets is the ideal platform for beginner and experienced real estate investors to connect and learn. Through podcasts, articles and books, Bigger Pockets helps investors discover new ways to boost their passive income through real estate. 

The best part is, a lot of the information is free! But if you want to dive deeper, you can become a member to access more exclusive deals and network with other members. They also have their own book publishing company, with resources like The Book on Rental Property Investing.

New real estate investing podcast episodes are released weekly on Thursdays, and the show is hosted by Brandon Turner and David Greene. Each episode features interviews with investors of various backgrounds, niches and experience levels. So one week, you might hear the story of how a waiter or stay-at-home mom made it big in real estate and the next week from a commercial real estate phenom who started with millions in capital. 

2. Think Realty Radio

Think Realty, hosted by Abhi Goldhar, educates and coaches real estate investors through audio and video content. This lets you choose how you want to experience the weekly episodes. Each show has great conversations between Abhi and his investor guests. He covers everything from financing to interior design. The show also focuses on the day-to-day operational and managerial functions for your real estate business. The episodes are short, about 30 minutes or less, so this show is easy to binge on or listen to weekly. 

3. The Real Estate Guys

Since 1997, professional investor Robert Helms and financial strategist Russell Gray have been hosting The Real Estate Guys Radio Show. It’s one of the most downloaded investing podcasts on iTunes. Each episode is fun, fast-paced, informative and all about real estate investing. 

Check Out:  A Landlord’s Guide to Finding and Managing Property Managers

The Guys also discuss investment strategy, economics, tax and asset protection planning, market and property due diligence, international diversification and more! The interviews feature notable experts, industry leaders and real estate investors. Listen to learn trends and how to avoid common and costly mistakes. With them, you can get started on your first real estate venture with confidence.

4. The Real Wealth Show

Author Kathy Fettke wrote the #1 bestseller Retire Rich with Rentals and has been a guest on CNN, CNBC, Fox News, NPR and CBS MarketWatch. On her podcast, Kathy shares practical strategies, market information and tips to help you acquire, manage and monetize your investments. 

The Real Wealth Show is a weekly podcast that discusses new opportunities for first-time and experienced investors. Kathy helps investors learn how to make money and build real wealth so they can live life on their own terms. Previous guests include Robert Kiyosaki, Loral Langemeier, Peter Schiff, John Burns and other real estate stars. Guests share their success stories and their costly mistakes.

5. Rental Rookie

Emily Du Plessis is the founder of Rental Rookie, a website and real estate investing podcast that teaches newbies how to invest in rental properties. Emily was a school teacher and got into real estate for the additional cash flow. As a result, she was able to quit her job and create more freedom in her life. Rental Rookie is a weekly podcast and a great place to learn the fundamentals of investing in rental properties. 

6. Real Estate Today Radio

This real estate investing podcast includes educational and practical tips real estate investors can use every day. It features content created by the National Association of Realtors®. The show covers different markets, property values and operational strategies that can be used for your investment company. This podcast is especially great for beginning investors because it’s fast-paced and packed with facts from experts.

7. Rental Income Podcast

Dan Lane is a relatable guy that started investing in real estate when he and his wife bought a new house and decided to rent their old home. Today, Dan owns six more properties. He created the Rental Income website and podcast to help people learn how to generate passive income and build long-term wealth through rental properties. 

This is a weekly podcast where Dan interviews landlords who share stories about how they built their rental portfolio. Guests also share issues they faced with property management, cash flow and managing tenants. So far, he’s spoken to over 200 landlords and the podcast content is great whether you’re going to buy your first rental or your 100th. 

 Podcasts for Flipping Houses

8. Escaping the REI Newbie Zone

Listen to Escaping the REI Newbie Zone to learn underground real estate secrets to help you build your business. Host Chris Bruce first discovered real estate in 2009 and became a full-time entrepreneur a year later. He started out wholesaling real estate deals. 

On the podcast, Chris helps newbies get beyond the first stage of their real estate business. He chose this niche because many people who get started in real estate investing don’t make it out of the newbie zone. His podcast tries to help new investors avoid trying every niche and buying every informative product. Listeners get top-notch strategies, tools and tricks to start flipping houses to create financial freedom.

Check Out:  Understand Your Student Loans And Tackle your Student Debt

9. Flipping Houses for Rookies

Flipping Houses for Rookies is a podcast that focuses on what you need to do to flip houses and get into real estate investing. Their website also includes courses to get beginners started. Podcast listeners learn where to find the money for deals and about lease options, buying options, wholesaling, selling houses, flipping houses and more. There is a new episode each week.

Podcasts for Multi-family Real Estate Investing

10. The Best Ever Show

Joe Fairless is a multi-family real estate investor who raised over $1 million from private investors for his first multi-family deal. Today, he controls over $900 million worth of real estate and hosts the longest-running daily real estate investing podcast. 

On the show, you can hear advice from successful real estate influencers. Joe focuses on apartment syndication and passive real estate investing, but he talks about other topics, too. Each episode includes solid advice without the fluff and actionable tips you can use immediately.

11. Lifetime CashFlow Through Real Estate Investing

On the Lifetime CashFlow through Real Estate Investing Podcast, host Rod Khleif gives listeners access to expert real estate investors, syndicators, lenders, property managers and more. Rod’s guest experts share their stories, tips and advice about how they built their businesses and fortunes through multi-family real estate investing.

Rod Khleif is a veteran real estate investor who has personally owned and managed over 2,000 apartments and homes. This is a great podcast for someone who wants to dive into multi-family real estate investing on their path to financial freedom.

12. The Old Capital Podcast

On The Old Capital Real Estate Investing Podcast, Michael Becker and Paul Peebles help multi-family real estate investors learn how to actively acquire and operate apartment complexes. In their interviews, you’ll find out how other investors are identifying, financing and operating multi-family properties. Use their helpful advice to get started and avoid common mistakes. A new episode is released every week. 

13. Apartment Building Investing with Michael Blank

Michael Blank is an author, investor and entrepreneur. He has been investing in residential and multi-family units since 2005 and started offering syndication deals in 2010. He has mastered the art of raising money for apartment deals and is teaching others how to do it, too. Through his company, he controls over $30 million in multi-family investments. Michael also contributes articles to Bigger Pockets regularly and has a blog.

On his podcast, Apartment Building Investing, Michael shares strategies for investors interested in commercial real estate investing. He specifically dives into how to acquire private financing for multifamily investing and syndication deals. 

14. Wheelbarrow Profits

Wheelbarrow Profits is hosted by Jake Stenziano and Gino Barbaro, a former drug rep and pizza guy (yep, Gino’s pizza). Jake and Gino achieved financial freedom and independence by first creating extra income through investment properties. Then, they opened a management company and later a capital company called Rand Capital. With multiple streams of income, they decided to syndicate properties, under Rand Partners, and they give away money through Rand Cares.

Check Out:  3 Ways Millennials Can Benefit From the Market Downturn

The two have several podcasts but focus on multi-family real estate investing most on Wheelbarrow Profits. Listen to this podcast if you want to learn more about increasing your cash flow and syndication. 

Interesting Real Estate Investing Podcasts Niches

There are plenty of ways to get involved in real estate investing. Some investors have built wealth by investing in less traditional markets. Check out these podcasts to learn more about real estate niches you might not know much about.

15. RETipster

RETipster is a podcast hosted by Seth Williams. Seth is very knowledgeable about a wide range of real estate investing niches. On the podcast, he helps beginners learn about everything in real estate from flipping houses to advanced tips and techniques like real estate investing website optimization. However, Seth’s expertise is in land investing, which requires less capital than traditional real estate investing.

16. The Mobile Home Park Investing Podcast

According to veteran real estate investors Kevin Bupp and Charles DeHart, mobile home park investing is a lucrative niche for passive income. On the podcast, they educate investors about the mobile home investing market and share valuable lessons they’ve learned along their journey. Occasionally, they have industry expert guests on the show, too. 

Tune in to find out why they think investing in mobile homes is more profitable than fix and flips, apartment investing, commercial real estate and more. 

Start Learning to Get Started or Get Better

As you can see, there are several ways to get involved in real estate investing. Fortunately, there are many real estate investing podcasts with experts to help you. Use these podcasts to learn more about real estate investing and adhere to the experts’ advice so you can minimize your mistakes. 

After you’ve heard a bit, get started. Don’t get stalled by information paralysis. Learn what you can, take note of things to watch out for, and go. Sure,you may make some mistakes, but you could be a great success, too. You’ll never know until you go.
